Topic: Pen Usability, Performance & Firmware Dependency


DOMAIN: Product

TOPIC_ID: T01

CANONICAL_TOPIC: Product Performance

SIGNAL: Neutral

TRAJECTORY: Stable


Content:

  • Peter has been testing the system end‑to‑end; core flows (org check, login, data flow, app log) work, but the pen still feels like a prototype rather than a product.
  • Usability/performance issues observed:
  • Touch lags and intermittent non‑responsiveness.
  • Touch offset on some screens (needing to tap on the edge of buttons), while other screens behave correctly.
  • Periods where the pen appears to “freeze” and then recovers after a few seconds.
  • Mandar fully agrees and notes some issues may be tied to the current firmware and thermal behavior (high temperature affecting screen performance, CPU throttling, or blocking due to Wi‑Fi or other processes).
  • Both agree to defer deep root‑cause work on these issues until the new firmware is available; remaining issues after that will need focused investigation.
  • Peter suggests using students to systematically use the pen and propose usability improvements, though Mandar cautions that some issues are firmware‑dependent.
  • Mandar describes prior work to reduce CPU utilization, particularly around the secondary camera.
  • Manasi led optimization efforts: initial changes reduced CPU load by ~40–50%; further work, guided by Mandar’s suggestions, identified the secondary camera implementation as the main cause.
  • By changing how the secondary camera is invoked/managed, CPU utilization for that path was reduced to near 0%, based on proper root‑cause analysis rather than ad‑hoc tweaks.
  • Peter acknowledges this as a significant improvement and links it to broader concerns about temperature and performance.

6. Risks / Issues

  • Known risks:
  • **Usability gap**: The pen currently feels prototype‑like; if not improved quickly after firmware update, it may undermine user acceptance of the MVP.
  • **Schedule compression**: Deferring multiple issues (usability, OCR, TTS) to post‑firmware while targeting a mid‑August freeze leaves limited time for discovery and remediation.
  • **TTS dependency on Vivoka**: Delayed response to Vivoka’s request for more information could push out any fixes they need to make, impacting TTS quality and architecture decisions.
  • **Information fragmentation**: Decisions and technical discussions are split between Teams and Atlassian; without disciplined linking, future traceability and audits will be difficult.
  • **Private channels**: Use of private Teams channels can hide misalignment and reduce leadership’s ability to detect early divergence from specifications.

  • Emerging concerns:
  • **Thermal and performance behavior**: Temperature‑related degradation and CPU throttling may persist even with new firmware if not explicitly addressed and tested.
  • **OCR engine quality**: If the casing issue is systemic, it may require vendor engagement or deeper algorithmic changes, which could be time‑consuming.
  • **Resource focus**: New resources and students are being onboarded while core firmware and TTS issues remain open; there is a risk of dilution of focus if not tightly managed.
  • **Post‑MVP process clarity**: Until the new release model is defined, there is a risk of ad‑hoc decisions and inconsistent handling of fixes vs new features.

7. Implications

  • Product:
  • The current pen experience is functionally rich but not yet at a quality level suitable for broad customer exposure; usability improvements will be critical to product perception.
  • Architectural decisions on TTS (on‑device vs server‑side) depend on Vivoka licensing; missteps could lead to rework or compliance issues.

  • Delivery:
  • Extending M11 and targeting a mid‑August freeze is feasible but leaves little slack; any slippage in firmware delivery or regression could impact downstream commitments.
  • The planned post‑MVP process work is essential to avoid chaos once the MVP is in the field and change requests start to accumulate.

  • Quality:
  • CPU and thermal optimizations (e.g., secondary camera) show the team can materially improve performance when focused; similar rigor will be needed for UI responsiveness and OCR.
  • Better integration of collaboration tools with Atlassian will improve traceability, which is important for quality management and future audits.

8. Alignment

  • Overall, decisions, actions, and direction are broadly aligned:
  • Everyone agrees on the need to move from prototype to product quality and to anchor work around the new firmware baseline.
  • The M11 extension and mid‑August freeze are consistent with the desire to stabilize before scaling.
  • The push for transparency and Atlassian‑centric knowledge capture aligns with the need for traceability and leadership oversight.

  • Gaps / inconsistencies:
  • Several critical items (TTS, OCR, usability) are being consciously deferred to a later window that is already time‑constrained; this creates a latent risk that is acknowledged but not yet mitigated with contingency plans.
  • The Teams channel is effective but still private; while Mandar is in the loop, broader transparency (e.g., for Mangesh, Theo) is aspirational rather than implemented.
  • The post‑MVP process is recognized as necessary but is still at the conceptual stage; until concrete rules and cadences are defined, execution risk remains.

9. Trend / Trajectory

  • Overall direction: **Improving**
  • Justification:
  • The team is actively addressing structural issues (CPU optimization, firmware update process, cross‑team coordination) and planning for post‑MVP governance.
  • Leadership is aligned on key concerns (usability, transparency, schedule) and is putting in place mechanisms (new meetings, tool integration exploration) to manage them.
  • However, improvement is contingent on successful and timely delivery of the new firmware and on closing open loops with external vendors (Vivoka); the trajectory is positive but fragile and requires disciplined follow‑through.
